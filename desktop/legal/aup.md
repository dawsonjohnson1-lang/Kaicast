## 1. INTRODUCTION

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") establishes the standards for appropriate use of the KaiCast platform, mobile application, and associated services (the "Service"). This AUP is incorporated into and forms part of KaiCast's Terms of Service. By using the Service, you agree to comply with this AUP.

KaiCast serves a community of divers, freedivers, snorkelers, and ocean enthusiasts. Maintaining a trustworthy, accurate, and respectful platform is essential for the safety and enjoyment of that community. We take policy violations seriously.

---

## 2. DIVE REPORT STANDARDS

Dive reports are the backbone of KaiCast's crowdsourced data quality. The following standards apply to all submitted reports.

### 2.1 Required Accuracy
You may only submit dive reports for dives, snorkels, or water activities you personally participated in or directly supervised. Reports must reflect your genuine firsthand observations. Do not submit reports for activities you did not personally witness.

### 2.2 Prohibited Report Content
You may not submit dive reports that:

(a) **Are fabricated or materially inaccurate** — including inflating visibility, misrepresenting conditions, or submitting reports for sites you did not visit;

(b) **Are submitted for commercial advantage** — including submitting artificially favorable reports to benefit a tour operator, dive shop, resort, or other business;

(c) **Are submitted for competitive harm** — including submitting artificially unfavorable reports to damage a competitor's reputation or discourage visits to a competing business's home site;

(d) **Contain spam, advertising, or promotional content** — dive reports are for conditions data, not marketing;

(e) **Contain personally identifiable information about other individuals** without their consent;

(f) **Contain graphic, violent, disturbing, or sexually explicit content**;

(g) **Disclose the location of protected, restricted, or sensitive marine species** (such as nesting sea turtles, Hawaiian monk seals, or juvenile reef sharks) in a manner that could enable harassment or disturbance; or

(h) **Disclose means of accessing restricted or protected marine areas** in violation of applicable law.

### 2.3 Photo Standards
Photos submitted with dive reports must be taken by you during the activity described in the report. Do not submit:
- Photos from other dives or other users without permission
- Digitally manipulated photos that misrepresent actual conditions
- Photos containing graphic content, nudity, or illegal activity

## 3. COMMUNITY CONDUCT

### 3.1 Respectful Interaction
When using community features (comments, ratings, messages), you must:
- Treat other users with respect and courtesy
- Provide constructive and honest feedback
- Base ratings and reviews on genuine experiences

### 3.2 Prohibited Conduct
You may not:

(a) **Harass, threaten, or intimidate** any other user, dive professional, or KaiCast staff;

(b) **Impersonate** any person or entity, including other users, certified dive instructors, marine wardens, or government officials;

(c) **Stalk, dox, or expose private information** about any individual;

(d) **Use hate speech** or post content that discriminates based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or national origin;

(e) **Coordinate harassment campaigns** against any individual or business;

(f) **Post content that glorifies or encourages** illegal activity, self-harm, or harm to marine life;

(g) **Solicit, recruit, or advertise** in a way not expressly permitted by KaiCast; or

(h) **Manipulate ratings, reviews, or report counts** through coordinated inauthentic behavior.

---

## 4. OCEAN SAFETY AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONDUCT

KaiCast serves a community whose activities carry inherent risk. You agree not to use the Service to:

(a) **Encourage unsafe diving practices**, including diving beyond your certification level, solo diving without appropriate safety measures, or ignoring clearly dangerous conditions;

(b) **Share information that facilitates illegal access** to protected marine areas, fish aggregating device (FAD) locations without authorization, or restricted government or military waters;

(c) **Facilitate illegal marine harvesting**, including sharing locations of protected species, disclosing active poaching sites, or coordinating illegal take;

(d) **Harass, chase, or disturb marine wildlife**, including posting content that depicts or encourages such activity; or

(e) **Damage coral reefs or other marine habitat**, including posting content that depicts standing on, breaking, or collecting coral.

## 5. PLATFORM INTEGRITY

### 5.1 Technical Restrictions
You may not:

(a) Use bots, scrapers, automated scripts, or any non-human means to access or extract data from the Service without KaiCast's prior written authorization;

(b) Attempt to reverse engineer, decompile, or derive source code from the Service, including KaiCast's scoring algorithms;

(c) Attempt to circumvent any access controls, rate limits, authentication systems, or security measures;

(d) Introduce malware, viruses, or malicious code into the Service;

(e) Conduct denial-of-service attacks or any activity that interferes with the availability of the Service; or

(f) Probe or test the vulnerability of KaiCast's systems without prior written authorization.

### 5.2 Account Integrity
You may not:

(a) Create multiple accounts to circumvent account suspensions or limitations;

(b) Buy, sell, or transfer KaiCast accounts;

(c) Use another user's account without their express permission; or

(d) Provide false information when creating or updating your account.

### 5.3 API and Data Use
Users with API access additionally may not:

(a) Exceed authorized usage quotas or rate limits;

(b) Use Condition Data in applications not authorized under your API license;

(c) Redistribute Condition Data without a redistribution license; or

(d) Use the API to power competitive condition scoring products.

## 7. ENFORCEMENT

### 7.1 Reporting Violations
To report a violation of this AUP, contact us at dawson@kaicast.com or use the in-app reporting feature on any post or report.

### 7.2 Enforcement Actions
KaiCast reserves the right to take any of the following actions in response to AUP violations, at our sole discretion:

- **Warning:** Issuance of a formal warning for first-time or minor violations
- **Content Removal:** Removal of non-compliant dive reports, photos, or other content
- **Feature Restriction:** Temporary removal of the ability to submit reports, post comments, or use specific features
- **Account Suspension:** Temporary suspension of account access
- **Account Termination:** Permanent ban from the Service for severe or repeated violations
- **Legal Action:** Referral to law enforcement or pursuit of civil remedies for illegal activity

### 7.3 Enforcement Principles
KaiCast will generally (but is not obligated to) follow a progressive enforcement approach:

| Violation Severity | Typical Response |
|---|---|
| Minor / first offense | Warning + content removal |
| Moderate / repeat minor | Feature restriction (7–30 days) |
| Serious (fabrication, harassment, impersonation) | Account suspension (30–90 days) or termination |
| Severe (illegal content, coordinated manipulation, threats) | Immediate permanent ban + possible law enforcement referral |

### 7.4 Appeals
If you believe an enforcement action was taken in error, you may submit an appeal within 14 days to dawson@kaicast.com with a description of the issue and any relevant context. We will review appeals within 10 business days. Our decision on appeal is final.

### 7.5 No Obligation to Monitor
KaiCast is not obligated to proactively monitor User Content for AUP compliance but reserves the right to do so. KaiCast is not liable for any User Content that violates this AUP.

## 8. DATA QUALITY PROGRAM

KaiCast may implement algorithmic and manual quality review of submitted dive reports to detect anomalies, inconsistencies, or indicators of fabrication. Users will be notified of enforcement actions but not necessarily of the specific signals that triggered quality review.
