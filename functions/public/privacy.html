1. INTRODUCTION

KaiCast operates an ocean conditions platform (the "Service") that aggregates environmental data and user-submitted dive reports to generate condition scores for coastal dive sites. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information about you when you use our Service.

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. By using the Service, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

2. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

Account Information: When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, password (stored in hashed form), and profile photo (if uploaded).

Dive Report Data: When you submit a dive report, we collect the information you choose to provide, which may include: dive site location, date and time, visibility rating, water temperature, current strength and direction, surge, marine life observations, surface conditions, depth, bottom time, reef health observations, overall conditions rating, photos, and free-text notes.

Communications: When you contact our support team or communicate with us through the Service, we collect the content of your communications.

Survey and Feedback Data: If you participate in surveys or provide feedback, we collect the information you submit.

Payment Information: When you purchase a subscription, we collect billing name and address. Payment card details are processed directly by our payment processor (Stripe, Inc.) and are not stored by KaiCast.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

Usage Data: We automatically collect information about how you interact with the Service, including pages viewed, features used, session duration, search queries, and actions taken.

Device Information: We collect information about the device you use to access the Service, including device type, operating system version, app version, unique device identifiers, and mobile network information.

Location Data: With your permission, we collect precise GPS location data to identify nearby dive sites and personalize your experience. You may disable location access through your device settings; note that this may limit certain features. We also collect approximate location from your IP address.

Log Data: Our servers automatically record information such as your IP address, browser type, referring URLs, and the date and time of requests.

Cookies and Similar Technologies: We use cookies and similar tracking technologies as described in our Cookie Policy.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

We may receive information about you from third parties, such as:

  • Social Login Providers: If you sign in using Google or Apple, we receive basic profile information (name, email, profile photo) from that provider, subject to your privacy settings with that provider.
  • Analytics Providers: We use analytics services to help understand how users interact with the Service.

3. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

Service Delivery: To create and manage your account, provide the features you request, process payments, and send transactional communications (account confirmations, subscription receipts, support responses).

Personalization: To customize your experience, including displaying relevant dive sites, condition scores, and dive reports based on your location and activity.

Service Improvement: To analyze how users use the Service, identify bugs and performance issues, and improve our features and algorithms. Dive report data may be used in anonymized and aggregated form to improve KaiCast's condition scoring models.

B2B Data Products: Aggregated and anonymized dive condition data may be incorporated into data products provided to hotel, resort, and tour operator partners. No personally identifiable information is shared with B2B partners without your explicit consent.

Safety and Security: To detect and prevent fraud, unauthorized access, abuse, and other harmful activity.

Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, respond to legal process, and protect our rights and the rights of others.

Marketing Communications: To send you promotional emails and in-app notifications about new features, special offers, and updates — but only if you have opted in. You may opt out at any time.

4. HOW WE SHARE YOUR INFORMATION

We do not sell your personal information. We share your information only in the following circumstances:

4.1 Service Providers. We share information with trusted third-party service providers who perform services on our behalf, including:

  • Firebase / Google Cloud Platform: Cloud infrastructure, database, and authentication
  • Stripe, Inc.: Payment processing
  • Resend (resend.com): Transactional and marketing email delivery
  • Firebase Analytics (Google LLC): Usage analytics

All service providers are contractually required to use your information only as directed by KaiCast and in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

4.2 B2B Partners. We share aggregated, anonymized data about dive conditions and platform usage with hotel, resort, and tour operator partners. This data does not contain information that identifies you personally.

4.3 Publicly Visible Content. Dive reports you submit are publicly visible on the Service by default, including your username, dive site, date, and conditions data. You may choose to submit reports anonymously where that feature is available.

4.4 Legal Requirements. We may disclose your information if required by law, subpoena, court order, or other legal process, or if we believe disclosure is necessary to protect the safety of any person, investigate fraud, or enforce our Terms of Service.

4.5 Business Transfers. If KaiCast is involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will provide notice before your personal information is transferred and becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

4.6 With Your Consent. We may share your information in other ways if you give us your explicit consent to do so.

5. YOUR RIGHTS AND CHOICES

5.1 Access and Portability. You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you, including your account data and submitted dive reports, in a portable format.

5.2 Correction. You may update or correct inaccurate account information at any time through your account settings, or by contacting us at dawson@kaicast.com.

5.3 Deletion. You may request deletion of your personal information by contacting us at dawson@kaicast.com. We will delete or anonymize your information within 30 days, subject to retention obligations described in Section 6 and legal holds.

5.4 Opt-Out of Marketing. You may opt out of marketing emails by clicking the "unsubscribe" link in any marketing email or by adjusting your notification settings in the app.

5.5 Location Data. You may disable location access at any time through your device settings.

5.6 California Residents (CCPA). If you are a California resident, you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, or sell;
  • Request deletion of your personal information;
  • Opt out of the sale of your personal information (we do not sell personal information);
  • Non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights.

To exercise your CCPA rights, contact us at dawson@kaicast.com. We will respond within 45 days.

5.7 Response Timeline. We will respond to all verifiable privacy requests within 30 days. If we require additional time, we will notify you.

6. DATA RETENTION

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to provide the Service and fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, subject to the following:

Data Type Retention Period
Account information Duration of account + 3 years post-deletion
Dive reports (public) Indefinite, unless you request deletion
Usage and analytics data 24 months rolling
Payment records 7 years (regulatory requirement)
Support communications 3 years
Server logs 90 days

After the applicable retention period, we will delete or anonymize your information.

7. DATA SECURITY

We implement technical and organizational security measures designed to protect your information against unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
  • Encryption of data at rest
  • Access controls limiting employee access to personal data on a need-to-know basis
  • Firebase Security Rules governing database access
  • Regular security assessments

No security system is impenetrable. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of your information. In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and applicable regulators as required by law.
