# KAICAST DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

**Effective Date:** [DATE]  
**Agreement Version:** 1.0.0

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is incorporated into and forms part of the agreement between KaiCast and Customer (the "Principal Agreement") and governs the processing of personal data where KaiCast acts as a data processor on behalf of Customer.

**KaiCast:** KaiCast, LLC ("KaiCast" / "Processor")  
**Customer:** [CUSTOMER LEGAL NAME] ("Customer" / "Controller")

If there is any conflict between this DPA and the Principal Agreement, this DPA shall prevail with respect to data processing matters.

---

## 1. DEFINITIONS

"**Applicable Data Protection Law**" means any applicable law or regulation relating to the processing of personal data, including without limitation the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the CPRA, and any other applicable US state privacy law or international law applicable to the Parties.

"**Controller**" means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data. Customer is the Controller with respect to Customer Personal Data.

"**Data Subject**" means an identified or identifiable natural person whose personal data is processed under this DPA.

"**Personal Data**" means any information that identifies or could be used to identify a Data Subject, as defined under Applicable Data Protection Law.

"**Personal Data Breach**" means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data.

"**Processing**" means any operation performed on Personal Data, including collection, recording, storage, adaptation, retrieval, use, disclosure, erasure, or destruction.

"**Processor**" means the entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. KaiCast is the Processor with respect to Customer Personal Data transmitted to KaiCast.

"**Sub-Processor**" means a third party engaged by KaiCast to process Personal Data on behalf of Customer.

---

## 2. SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

This DPA applies when Customer transmits Personal Data about Customer's end users, guests, employees, or other individuals to KaiCast in connection with the Principal Agreement. This DPA does not apply to:

(a) Data that has been fully anonymized or aggregated such that individuals cannot be identified; or  
(b) Data that KaiCast independently collects directly from end users under KaiCast's own privacy policy.

The Parties acknowledge that under the standard Platform integration contemplated by the Principal Agreement, it is not anticipated that Customer will transmit Personal Data about its guests to KaiCast. This DPA governs any scenario in which such transmission occurs (e.g., if Customer uses an API integration that includes end user identifiers).

## 4. CUSTOMER OBLIGATIONS (CONTROLLER)

Customer represents and warrants that:

(a) Customer has a valid legal basis under Applicable Data Protection Law for sharing Personal Data with KaiCast and for authorizing KaiCast to process such data as described in this DPA;

(b) Customer has provided all required notices to Data Subjects regarding the processing of their Personal Data by KaiCast as a service provider;

(c) Customer will comply with all Applicable Data Protection Laws applicable to it as a Controller; and

(d) Customer's instructions for processing Personal Data are and will remain lawful.

---

## 5. KAICAST OBLIGATIONS (PROCESSOR)

KaiCast agrees to:

**5.1 Instructions.** Process Personal Data only on Customer's documented instructions, including as set forth in this DPA and the Principal Agreement, unless required by law. KaiCast will promptly inform Customer if, in KaiCast's opinion, an instruction violates Applicable Data Protection Law.

**5.2 Confidentiality.** Ensure that personnel authorized to process Personal Data are subject to binding confidentiality obligations.

**5.3 Security.** Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect Personal Data, as described in Schedule B.

**5.4 Sub-Processors.** Engage Sub-Processors only as set forth in Section 6 and Schedule A.

**5.5 Data Subject Rights.** Promptly notify Customer of any Data Subject requests relating to Customer Personal Data received directly by KaiCast, and provide reasonable assistance to Customer in fulfilling such requests.

**5.6 Data Protection Impact Assessments.** Provide reasonable assistance to Customer in connection with any data protection impact assessments required by Applicable Data Protection Law.

**5.7 No Sale.** KaiCast will not sell, share, or disclose Customer Personal Data to third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising or any purpose other than providing the services under the Principal Agreement.

**5.8 Return or Deletion.** Upon termination of the Principal Agreement, at Customer's written request, KaiCast will delete or return Customer Personal Data within 30 days, unless retention is required by law.

---

## 6. SUB-PROCESSORS

**6.1 Authorized Sub-Processors.** Customer authorizes KaiCast to engage the Sub-Processors listed in Schedule A. KaiCast will ensure Sub-Processors are bound by data protection obligations substantially equivalent to those in this DPA.

**6.2 New Sub-Processors.** KaiCast will provide Customer with at least **30 days' prior written notice** before engaging any new Sub-Processor. Customer may object to a new Sub-Processor within 15 days of notice on reasonable data protection grounds. If the Parties cannot resolve the objection, Customer may terminate the Principal Agreement for cause with 30 days' notice.

**6.3 Liability.** KaiCast remains liable to Customer for the acts and omissions of its Sub-Processors to the same extent KaiCast would be liable if it performed the processing directly.

---

## 7. DATA BREACH NOTIFICATION

**7.1 Notification.** KaiCast will notify Customer without undue delay, and in any event within **72 hours** of becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Personal Data. Notification will be provided to the contact designated by Customer in writing.

**7.2 Contents.** To the extent available, KaiCast's notification will include: (a) a description of the nature of the breach; (b) the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected; (c) the categories and approximate number of records affected; (d) the likely consequences of the breach; and (e) measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

**7.3 Investigation.** KaiCast will cooperate with Customer's reasonable investigation of any Personal Data Breach and provide updates as additional information becomes available.

---

## 8. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

Personal Data processed under this DPA is stored and processed in the United States. If the Principal Agreement expands to cover Data Subjects in jurisdictions with data transfer restrictions (such as the European Economic Area), the Parties will execute appropriate transfer mechanisms (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) prior to any such transfer.

---

## 9. RECORDS OF PROCESSING

KaiCast will maintain reasonable records of processing activities conducted under this DPA, including the categories of data processed, Sub-Processors engaged, and security measures implemented, and will make such records available to Customer upon reasonable written request.

---

## 10. AUDIT RIGHTS

**10.1 Audits.** Customer may, upon 30 days' written notice and no more than once per year, request that KaiCast make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA. Such information may be provided via documentation, certifications, or questionnaires rather than on-site audits.

**10.2 On-Site Audits.** Physical on-site audits of KaiCast's facilities are permitted only where Customer demonstrates a specific, documented compliance concern that cannot be adequately addressed through documentation alone, and are subject to KaiCast's reasonable security and confidentiality requirements.

---

## 11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

The total liability of either Party under this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability provisions in the Principal Agreement.

## SCHEDULE B — TECHNICAL AND ORGANIZATIONAL SECURITY MEASURES

KaiCast implements the following security measures:

**Access Controls**
- Role-based access control limiting personnel access to Personal Data on a need-to-know basis
- Multi-factor authentication required for administrative access to production systems
- Access logs maintained for production database and infrastructure

**Data Encryption**
- Encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Encryption of data at rest using AES-256 or equivalent
- Encrypted storage of authentication credentials (bcrypt hashing for passwords)

**Infrastructure Security**
- Hosted on Google Cloud Platform with Firebase Security Rules governing database access
- Network security controls including firewalls and VPC configuration
- Regular security patching of infrastructure components

**Incident Response**
- Documented incident response plan
- Security incident log maintained
- Personnel trained on data breach identification and escalation

**Physical Security**
- Relies on Google Cloud Platform's physical security controls for data center security

**Vendor Management**
- Sub-Processors reviewed for data protection compliance before engagement
- Data processing agreements in place with Sub-Processors
